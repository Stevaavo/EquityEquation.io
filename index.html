The Graham Equation


...is a mathematical method developed by Paul Graham for determining a reasonable portion of equity to offer an employee or investor.

The Graham equation involves the 4 variables below. By providing any 3 of these numbers, you can solve for the 4th number.

Advanced Parameters

These parameters are used in the equation. I’m using Paul Graham’s recommendations by default, but feel free to change them.

Salary Multiplier

An employee’s salary is paid every year, not just once. In addition, the company will incur additional “overhead” costs from the new hire. This multiplier accounts for those extra costs.

Company's Profit

If you set this to zero, the Graham Equation would tell you how much equity an employee should receive in order to leave the company neither any better nor worse off than they were before they hired him. When you don’t account for profit, the Graham Equation calculates the “break even” number. Obviously, no company is interested in merely breaking even on a new hire, so this parameter specifies how much profit they want to make on their new hire.
